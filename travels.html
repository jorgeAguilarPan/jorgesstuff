Smoky Mountains

June 2021 - The family took a two week trip to the smoky mountans. I joined them a couple of days after they arrived. We stayed in Mars Hill, a very small rural town about half an hour outside of Asheville. I'd been in the area before, but this time was a lot more memorable.
Driving the Blue Ridge Highway had been on my list for sometime and this was my first time in the area since learning to drive. I wasted no time.
The Blue Ridege Mountains are truly spectacular. The area is covered in temperate rainforests and the humidity remains very high year round. This is most evident in the early mornings. Reaching the nearest highway entrance around 4-5 am, the fog is so thick that visibility is extremely limited (to maybe about 5-10 meters). Beyond that distance, the path becomes totally obscured. Driving faster than 50 km/h is quite dangerous. The feeling is amazing, however. Nothing quite compares to driving and walking inside the clouds. Several hiking trails straddle the length of the highway. The highest point on the highway is not too far from Mars Hill.
The only disappointment of this trip was I was still unable to find any pawpaws.





La Gaspésie

August 2021 - Took a break from college for the year. Things got boring pretty quickly. August came around and while everybody was starting school I decided to hit the road with Blondie. We packed the car and set off with no particular destination in mind. As luck would have it, Canada decided to open its borders to Americans starting on August 9th, 2021. We hitched a left near Toledo and crossed into Windsor just after midnight on the 9th. We kept heading East from there.
We reached Québec city and stayed stayed on the Southern coast of the St. Lawrence. Things got really interesting around here. The Gaspésie is nowhere near as populous as Upper Québec; and this was immediately noticeable. Services are also very widely spread apart. The car out of gas near Rivière-du-Loup and we had to wait until morning to fill up. We followed the road sticking to the coast further West. The coast gets pretty jagged and mountainous with steep cliffs facing the water and towns.
Eventually we arrived at Forillon National Park. The peninsula narrows and the road ends. The last three and a half kilometers are an upward hike towards the End of the World (Le but du Monde). While the plan was to camp out here or in the vicinity we were forced to turn back early to make my citizenship ceremony back in Chicago. We continued on to Percé just under an hour down the road. We stopped here for a day. Watching the sun rise by the rock was truly spectacular.
After this, we started the trip back home. We straddled the coast until we reached Moncton and the followed the fastest road back home. Arriving in Ohio we made the decision to visit a friend studying at Miami.
